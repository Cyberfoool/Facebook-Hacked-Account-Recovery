Scenario 3: You've lost your email and phone

If all your contact info was removed, follow these steps:

  1. Backup Publicly Accessible Data: Start by saving any public posts, photos, or profile details from your hacked account using Apify's Facebook Scraper. This step preserves a record of your account's visible content, which can help prove it's yours and protect important memories or evidence.
  2. Lock Out the Attacker: Try to disrupt the hacker's access by entering wrong passwords several times or reporting your account as hacked at facebook.com/hacked. You can also use a VPN to log in from a different location, which might trigger Facebook's security to lock the account and block the attacker.
  3. Mimicking the Account: If you have clues about the attacker's login (like their location or browser from your facebook security email), use tools like User-Agent Switcher and a VPN to copy their setup. This can trick Facebook into thinking you're the same user, giving you a chance to access recovery options.
  4. Leverage Instagram's Back Channel: Create or update an Instagram account with the same email or phone number as your Facebook, then sign up for Meta Verified to get live support. Contact Meta's team through Instagram to explain the hack and ask them to remove the attacker's changes.
  5. Legal Interventions: If all else fails, hire a lawyer to send a formal complaint to Meta's legal team, detailing the hack and its impact. This can pressure Facebook to act quickly, especially for important accounts like business pages, though it may take time and money.
